## Origin (why TT exists)

Timed Trading started as a way to **stop drowning in manual prep**.

Every session (and every weekend) meant sorting through countless tickers—charts, levels, “is this ready to enter or exit?”—while social and paid research kept surfacing **new names not yet on the list**. Attention couldn’t keep up; **moves were missed**.

The first breakthrough was **codifying that analysis** so every ticker could sit on one view: a **quadrant / bubble map** (momentum × trend). Suddenly cohorts were visible—who was aligned, who was in Pullback, who was in a Setup zone—instead of one ticker at a time in a spreadsheet.

That visualization changed how trading was approached and opened a bigger question: if analysis can be codified, **what else can be automated** so energy goes into **strategy, backtest, and calibration**—not repetitive scanning?

TT is built for someone like the founder: a retail operator who wants repetitive work automated and outcomes improving over time. The **model is the centerpiece**; the UI is deliberately simple—**lanes, levels, follow the lifecycle** (Setup → Trim → Exit for Active Trader; Buy Zone → Reduce for Investor).

## Trading philosophy (what the product encodes)

- **Flywheel:** day trading fuels swing trading fuels investing—all three in parallel. The system should support **short-term trades on names also held long-term**, because markets oscillate; positions can too.
- **Edge is timing, not stock picking:** not “NVDA is a great company,” but **when to accumulate, when to swing, when to Trim, when to Exit, what to avoid**.
- **One move, many instruments:** the signal is the **move from beginning to end**; the follower chooses shares, options, spreads, etc.
- **No guru positioning:** not claiming to find the next 10-bagger or that only elites can make money. The separator is **how motivations, emotions, and failures are handled**—fear and greed, Mr. Hyde in the trader chair.
- **Discipline as a service:** an emotionless model guides each action so the human doesn’t improvise under stress. Wins and losses feed **lessons** for the next trade—not euphoria, not paralysis, **keep moving**.

## Builder lens (tone)

- Career building **digital products**; TT is a product company, not a Discord signals room.
- Honest constraint: would share TT for free if infrastructure cost didn’t exist—**pricing offsets build/run cost**, not “get rich from subscribers.”
- Future north star: **delegated execution** (e.g. broker/agent integrations)—early work exists; **not marketed until proven**. Today’s promise: **structure + signals + ledger**, not autopilot wealth.

## Competitive frame (how to talk about others)

- Landscape: Substack-style writeups, manual Discord alerts, research portals (e.g. Tenet-style “where we stand”)—often **strong personalities, high price**, **manual** delivery.
- TT difference: **kanban lanes of automated lifecycle signals** (entry → trim → exit), **bubble map** for the whole universe, **public proof ledger**, model that **learns from its own trades**.
- Do **not** name competitors in posts. Compare on **features**: manual alert vs lifecycle, entry-only vs trim/exit, narrative vs logged receipts.

## Pricing philosophy (founder intent)

- **$60/mo** feels fair vs the landscape—and sometimes **cheap** if the process is real; **$29** might widen the funnel for the target retail user.
- **Charter** was scarcity for early believers + direct line to the team—not “fake limited” for conversion spam. Prefer **founding member** framing over countdown pressure.
- **Do not rush price cuts for revenue.** Trust and proof come first; test **$60 + 30-day trial** before introducing a lower tier.

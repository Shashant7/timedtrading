How Timed Trading works — the 60-second mental model

One paragraph before the deep dive: Timed Trading is a model that watches a curated list of stocks, scores them across multiple timeframes every five minutes, decides when conditions actually line up for a trade, and then manages that trade through a clear lifecycle. You see exactly what it’s thinking, exactly when something changes, and you can follow along or use the same read to position your own way.

The four-step loop

  1. Universe — we seed a focused list of tickers (the TT Universe) based on what’s actually moving in the current market cycle. You can add your own on top of it.
  2. Scoring — every ticker is scored across 8+ timeframes every 5 minutes. The model reads trend, momentum, structure, and confluence.
  3. Decision — when a setup actually qualifies, the AI CIO validates it, then it gets placed in the right kanban lane with explicit entry, stop, and target levels.
  4. Management — from the moment a trade is open, the model moves it through lanes (Hold → Defend → Trim → Exit) based on what the data is doing in real time.
The mental shift: Timed Trading isn’t a chart-reading shortcut. It’s a system that watches more tickers than you can, more carefully than you can, and only surfaces the ones that pass its filters. Your job is to read the lanes, not the charts.

The TT Universe — what we watch and why

The universe is a deliberately curated list, not the whole market. Watching everything means watching nothing well. We focus on the names that actually move and re-balance as cycles shift.

TT Selected tickers

Every ticker we seed gets a TT Selected indicator in the dashboard. This is our shortlist of names the model has identified as potential high-performers in the current market cycle — trending mega-caps, sector leaders, high-momentum mid-caps, theme plays, and macro proxies (commodities, defense, AI infra, etc.).

The universe is not static. As market regimes shift — energy rolls over, AI rotates back in, defense breaks out — we add, remove, or re-weight tickers to match what’s actually moving.

Universe-change alerts

Every time the universe is refreshed, a user-facing alert fires. You see exactly what changed:

  • Added — new tickers entering the universe, with our rationale
  • Removed — tickers we’re no longer tracking (e.g. M&A, regime change)
  • Boosted weighting — existing tickers we’re upgrading
  • Lowered weighting — existing tickers we’re downgrading

These alerts are tied to two events: ETF rebalance activity, where we mirror systematic shifts in major sector ETFs, and admin uplift, when we promote tickers from the broader watchlist (we call this Upticks) into the active universe.

Your own tickers

You can add tickers outside the seeded list, with a soft limit applied to keep your dashboard focused. Any ticker you add gets the same scoring and analysis the model runs on TT Selected names.

One nuance: the model needs historical data to evaluate a new ticker properly. When you first add one, you’ll see scoring populate within the next hour as we backfill bars, but a full read (across 8 timeframes with multi-week structure) takes a full session to mature. Once data is harvested, you get the full model analysis — same lanes, same triggers.

The Daily Brief — how the model frames the session

Every trading day, before the open, the model publishes a Daily Brief. This is your two-minute orientation: what the market is, what’s driving it, and what the model is doing with its open positions.

What’s inside

  • Regime & market state — risk-on, risk-off, or transitional? Are major indices stacked bull or bear on the daily?
  • What’s driving price action — macro events, sector rotation, earnings tape, technical catalysts
  • Index predictions — SPY and QQQ key levels for the session, ATR-based expected range, and game plan
  • Open position guidance — for every trade the model has open, the brief tells you whether to hold, defend, trim, or exit, and why
  • Setups in formation — tickers that aren’t triggers yet but are building toward one
How to use it: read the brief at the start of your session. It gives you the same framing the model is using to make its own decisions. If you only have two minutes before the open, this is the page to read.

Two modes, one universe — Active Trader vs Investor

Active Trader and Investor share the same ticker universe but score and act on it differently. Same data, different time horizons, different triggers. You decide which mode (or both) fits your style.

Active Trader

Short to medium swings

The model is looking for setups that resolve in 1 to 10 trading days, occasionally longer when momentum stays intact. These are swing trades meant to hold overnight but exit decisively when the setup is done.

Horizon
1–10 days (rarely 30+)
Direction
LONG or SHORT
Trigger
Momentum, pullback, gap reversal, continuation, range reversal
Exit
SL hit, TP1/TP2/TP3, doctrine, time-stop, thesis flip
Investor

Multi-week to multi-month

The model is looking for tickers transitioning into accumulation zones or holding strong trends. These are positions meant to last weeks, months, or longer. When a ticker enters Accumulate or Core Hold, that’s when Investor mode adds to it.

Horizon
Weeks to many months
Direction
LONG-only (currently)
Trigger
Accumulation zone entry, trend health, RS new high
Exit
Reduce signal, trend break, structural failure

You’re not locked to following the model exactly

When the model takes a trade, you have options. You can:

  • Follow along directly — buy or short the same shares the model is using
  • Position for the same move via options — calls or puts on the same direction
  • Position via leveraged ETFs — TQQQ for QQQ moves, SOXL for semis, etc.
  • Use the read but size differently for your own risk tolerance
Coming soon: we’re building dedicated play paths into the app so each trade card will surface the matching options strike, leveraged ETF, or futures contract for whoever wants to play the same move a different way.

Active Trader kanban lanes — what each one means and how to act

Every ticker in Active Trader mode sits in exactly one lane at a time. The lane tells you where the ticker is in its lifecycle and what action (if any) the model is taking.

👁 Watch
The model is tracking this ticker but no setup is forming yet. Price is sitting between meaningful levels, scores are neutral. Background context only.
Just monitor
🔮 Setup
A pattern is building — pullback into a key zone, structure forming, momentum returning. Not a trade yet, but worth keeping in your peripheral vision.
Stalk
🎯 Enter / Just Entered
A setup has qualified. The AI CIO is doing its final check, or the trade has just been initiated. Entry, SL, and TP1/TP2/TP3 are all defined — you can size and join the trade right here.
Position now
📚 Hold
Position is working as expected. No warning signals, no targets hit yet. Trust the plan — the model is letting it run.
Hold
🛡️ Defend
The trade is under pressure — adverse P&L, RSI weakening, phase against, or below the trigger. The model is tightening the stop and protecting capital, not exiting.
Tighten / wait
✂ Trim
The trade hit a partial profit target (TP1 or TP2) or a doctrine trim signal fired. The model is locking in gains while keeping a runner on for the full target.
Take partial
❌ Exit
Close it now. Either the SL was hit, the final target was reached, a doctrine exit fired (thesis flip, time stop, macro risk), or the structure broke. This is the most urgent lane — act fast.
Exit immediately
How transitions work: A ticker moves between lanes in real time as the model re-scores. A Setup can become an Enter within five minutes if the trigger fires. A Hold can become a Defend if RSI weakens. A Defend can become a Trim or back to Hold depending on what price does. The card itself never moves — you scroll through lanes to see what’s where.

Investor kanban lanes — the long-game equivalent

Investor mode uses different lanes because the time horizon is fundamentally different. A Hold in Active Trader might last 3 days. A Core Hold in Investor mode might last 6 months. Same universe, different rhythm.

📈 Accumulate
Strong score with the ticker sitting in a favorable accumulation zone. The model is building or adding to this position. Healthy trend + actionable price.
Consider buying
📊 Core Hold
Position is working. Trend stays intact, relative strength is solid, no structural concerns. The model holds and lets it compound.
Hold
👁 Watch
Signals are mixed. Score is moderate, trend is uncertain, price is neither in a buying zone nor showing weakness. Wait for a directional break.
Wait and monitor
📉 Reduce
The ticker is showing weakness. Trend rolling, relative strength fading. The model is trimming or fully exiting the position.
Consider trimming
🔍 On Watch
Moderate score, worth tracking. Not actionable yet, but a few good sessions could move it into Accumulate.
On the radar
📋 Low Conviction
Weak read overall. The model isn’t building a thesis here — shown for completeness so you know where the rest of the universe sits.
Skip for now
⛔ Avoid
Cautionary. Trend broken, weak relative strength, structural concerns. The model is not interested in long-term exposure here.
Skip
Action Board vs Research lanes: the first four lanes (Accumulate, Core Hold, Watch, Reduce) are the Action Board — tickers that need attention. On Watch / Low Conviction / Avoid are Research lanes — the rest of the universe, parked for context.

Anatomy of a kanban card — reading every element

Each kanban card packs a lot of information into a small space. Here’s every element, what it means, and how to read it — for both modes.

Active Trader card

1Move-status emoji — 🟢 active, ✅ target achieved, ⛔ thesis invalidated.
2Ticker symbol — the stock. Click anywhere on the card to open it in the right rail.
3Quality badges — 🏆 multi-confluence, 💡 unique setup, ⭐ user-saved, and others.
4Status pill — Prime (highest conviction), Eligible, or Watch.
5Direction pill — L for LONG, S for SHORT. Card outline color matches.
6Horizon chip — ≤7d short-term, 8–30d swing, 31d+ longer hold.
7Sparkline — the last several bars of price action at a glance.
8Live price — current quote (or last close after-hours).
9Day change — intraday move, % and dollar amount.
10Score — the model’s composite score across all timeframes (0–100).
11Rank — where this ticker sits in the universe today.
12RR — reward-to-risk ratio (potential gain ÷ potential loss). Higher is better.
13SL — the stop-loss price. Red means “exit if hit.”
14TP — the next take-profit target (TP1 → TP2 → TP3 progression).
15ETA — the model’s estimate of how many days to target.
16Phase & Completion bars — Phase = where price is between SL and TP; Completion = how far the setup has matured. Markers tell you where SL/TP sit relative to current price.
17Lane indicator — this card’s current lane. When it changes, the card pulses.

Investor card

ASave star — click to add this ticker to your Saved list (☆ → ★).
BTicker + company name — symbol and full name. Click to open in right rail.
CPrice — current quote (or last close).
DDay change — intraday %. Green up, red down.
EScore — investor composite (0–100). Color-coded: green ≥70, amber 50–69, red <50.
FSignal tags — ZONE = price in accumulation zone, RS HIGH = relative-strength new high. Strong corroborating signals.
GStage — which Investor lane this ticker is in. Left accent stripe matches lane color.

How we keep score — transparency, self-learning, AI CIO

Every trade the model takes is recorded, scored, and reviewed. Win or loss. There’s no hidden cherry-picking. The system learns from its own results and the AI CIO actively reviews each entry decision against historical context.

Full transparency

Every trade in Active Trader and Investor mode is visible in the Trade Ledger — entry, exit, P&L, why it triggered, and why it exited. Wins, losses, scratches, every one. This is the truth check on the model. If you ever wonder “is this thing actually working?” — the ledger answers it.

Setup-level performance

Trades are tagged by setup family: gap reversal, momentum, pullback, continuation, range reversal, ATH breakout, n-test support, and others. We track win rate and expectancy for each one over time. When a setup’s expectancy drops, we throttle it. When a setup runs hot, we let it work.

Weekly retrospective

Every week, the model runs a retrospective on its own trades. What setups paid? Which tickers misbehaved? Where did the AI CIO save us, and where did it block a winner? The findings are stored in memory and actively consulted by the AI CIO when the next entry qualifies.

The AI CIO

The Agent-in-the-Loop CIO (Chief Investment Officer) is a final review layer before any entry is taken. It checks the proposed trade against:

  • The current regime and risk environment
  • Recent results for the same setup family and ticker
  • Concentration limits (sector, direction)
  • Open positions and overall portfolio shape
  • Macro events on the calendar (CPI, FOMC, earnings)

If the CIO approves, the trade enters. If it blocks, the card stays in Setup with a __cio_blocked reason you can read. Either way, the decision is recorded.

Why this matters: a model that says “trust me” without showing its work is just a black box. Timed Trading is the opposite — every signal, every entry, every exit is logged and reviewable. The system improves because it has to face its own results every week.

You now have the vocabulary. Open the dashboard, find a card in the Enter lane, and read it the way the model reads it. The rest is just practice.

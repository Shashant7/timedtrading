Getting started

Timed Trading is a scoring and timing system that watches a curated set of US stocks, ETFs, and crypto, then surfaces explicit entries, stops, take-profit targets, and lifecycle actions with clear reasoning for each move. Every signal is reviewed by an AI Chief Investment Officer before it lands as a tradeable action.

The same engine drives two horizons against one universe:

  • Active Trader — multi-timeframe entries with stop-losses, take-profit ladders, trims, and trailing logic. LONG and SHORT. Held days to weeks.
  • Investor (Trend-Hold) — LONG-only accumulation expressed in shares or LEAPs. One decision per ticker per day. Held weeks to months.

Read the full walkthrough on the How it works page, or jump to the live trade ledger at /proof.html.

Four steps, repeated every five minutes during market hours:

  • Universe. A curated TT Selected list rotates with the cycle. Subscribers can add their own tickers; backfill plus 8-timeframe scoring runs automatically.
  • Scoring. Each ticker is scored 0–100 across eight timeframes (5-minute through monthly), then assigned a conviction tier, R-multiple, rank, and dual Trader/Investor classification.
  • Decision. Setups are routed through kanban lanes (Watch → Setup → Enter → Hold → Defend → Trim → Exit for Active Trader; Accumulate / Core Hold / Watch / Reduce for Investor). Each entry candidate is reviewed by the AI CIO and either approved or blocked with a recorded reason.
  • Management. Once a trade is live, the lane drives the management: SL hit, TP1 trim, runner trail, full exit. Every transition is logged in the trade ledger.

The mental shift Timed Trading asks for: stop reading charts; start reading lanes.

The Active Strategy & TT Universe

The Active Strategy is the system's editorial macro view of what is likely to happen next in the current market regime: the dominant phase, scenario probabilities, sector tilts, tier-1 themes, and the risks to watch. Think of it as the Fundstrat-style playbook.

It feeds four downstream surfaces:

  • The AI CIO uses it as macro context when approving or blocking entries.
  • The Daily Brief opens with the playbook's current scenario weights.
  • Discovery scoring tilts towards tier-1 themes and away from risk-off names.
  • The Right Rail tags every ticker as ON-THESIS or OFF-THESIS against the current playbook.

It's not a buy list. It's the lens the rest of the engine looks through.

The TT Selected list is the system's curated working universe: ~30–60 US stocks & ETFs across all 11 GICS sectors, plus BTCUSD and ETHUSD. It rotates with cycles — tickers are added, removed, boosted, or lowered based on momentum, sector rotation, ETF rebalances, and the Upticks promotion queue.

Every change generates a universe-change alert with rationale so subscribers can see why a name entered or left.

Daily Brief, AI CIO & Two Modes

The Daily Brief is the pre-market orientation document. Each morning it covers:

  • The current market regime + Active Strategy scenario weights.
  • SPY / QQQ levels, ATR ranges, and overnight catalysts.
  • Open-position guidance from the model (which trades to defend, which to trim).
  • Setups forming — what to watch the open for.
  • The ES-style narrative: how the session is likely to play out from the open to close.

An Evening Brief publishes after the close with a session recap and overnight setup watch.

The AI Chief Investment Officer is an agent-in-the-loop validation layer. Before any Active Trader entry goes live, the CIO reviews the proposed trade against:

  • Current regime + Active Strategy alignment.
  • Recent performance of the setup family (Pullback · Continuation · Reversal · etc.).
  • Concentration limits across open positions.
  • Calendar events (earnings, macro, dividends).
  • The 8-layer Root Strategy confluence verdict (RIDE / READY / DRIFT / FADE).

The CIO returns one of APPROVE, ADJUST, or BLOCK with a recorded reason. Blocked setups stay in the Setup lane with the reason visible to the operator. Every decision is auditable in Insights → AI CIO Decisions.

Same scoring engine, different cadence and horizon.

  • Active Trader — 1–10 day swings, LONG and SHORT. Trade-managed by lane: Watch → Setup → Enter / Just Entered → Hold → Defend → Trim → Exit. Position bar on every card shows SL / Entry / TP1.
  • Investor — weeks to months, LONG-only. Four action lanes (Accumulate, Core Hold, Watch, Reduce) and three research lanes (On Watch, Low Conviction, Avoid). One decision per ticker per day. Expressed as shares or as a deep-ITM LEAP option play.

The Right Rail of every ticker shows both the Trader and Investor bias at once, so an operator can see "this looks long for the swing AND core-hold for the long term."

Options & LEAPs

Yes. Every long-direction ticker carries an Options tab in the Right Rail with a strategy ladder ranked for the configured risk profile: Long Call, Vertical Spread, LEAP, Moonshot (when confluence + momentum activate), Cash-Secured Put, Covered Call, and a plain stock fallback. Each play shows strikes, expiration, premium, max loss, max gain at target, and breakeven.

The Today page also surfaces the day's top "Options Plays" as a sidebar so subscribers can browse the highest-conviction expressions across the universe.

LEAPs are the natural option expression of an Investor thesis. The engine emits a deep-ITM (~0.80 delta) LEAP roughly 18 months out as the headline play for any Investor-stage entry. The rationale follows the stock-replacement framework: synthetic long exposure at a fraction of the capital, defined max loss, no margin call, no forced exit on drawdown. Each LEAP card includes a roll target (T-180 days), a poor-man's covered call suggestion, an IV-at-entry caveat, and a capital-efficiency assessment.

Trader-mode tickers also list the LEAP in the ladder as a "long-term thesis alternative" below the short-dated primary. The Right Rail has a Horizon toggle so subscribers can preview either expression on any ticker.

Performance & Proof

Live, public, and updated automatically: timed-trading.com/proof.

The proof page pulls real-time figures from the simulated trade ledger — equity curve, Sharpe, max drawdown, win rate, average winner vs average loser, every closed trade with reasoning. No screenshots, no cherry-picking.

Past performance is simulated. The ledger is paper-traded against the model's signals; it is not a guarantee of future returns, and live execution introduces slippage, fills, and broker behavior that the simulation doesn't model. The transparency is the point: every trade can be reviewed.

A paper-traded portfolio that executes the model's signals as they fire — entries, trims, take-profits, stop-losses, exits. The ledger lives at the Portfolio page (subscriber view) and at /proof.html (public view), and it's the record of how the system actually behaves over time. Both Active Trader and Investor modes maintain their own ledger.

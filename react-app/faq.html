Everything you need to know about Timed Trading

General

Timed Trading is a scoring and timing system for swing and position traders. It watches a curated set of tickers positioned to outperform in the current market, scores them across 8 timeframes, and provides clear levels for support, resistance, and targets — along with precise timing on when to act. The goal is simple: stop guessing and start trading with conviction.

The system tracks a curated universe of tickers that are rotated periodically to match the current market environment. Coverage includes:

  • US Stocks & ETFs — Large-caps (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, etc.), sector ETFs (XLF, XLE, XLK), and high-potential names across all 11 GICS sectors.
  • Crypto — Bitcoin (BTCUSD) and Ethereum (ETHUSD).

The universe isn't fixed at a static number. Tickers are added and removed based on where the strongest opportunities are forming, drawing from momentum screens and sector rotation analysis.

The system draws from curated screening processes to identify names that are positioned to outperform. The universe is not static — it rotates periodically as market conditions shift, so the focus stays on what matters now.

Scoring & Signals

Each ticker receives a composite score (0–100) computed across 8 timeframes — from 5-minute bars up to monthly charts. The engine evaluates trend direction, momentum strength, volume profile, and risk/reward ratios. Higher scores indicate stronger conviction setups. Scores refresh every 5 minutes during market hours.

It's the three-phase timing process at the core of the system:

  • Ready — A ticker enters the Setup lane. The system detects early signs of a potential move forming.
  • Aim — Levels lock in. Support, resistance, and targets are defined with clear stop-loss and take-profit zones.
  • Fire — The ticker moves to Enter. Conditions align, and the system signals it's time to act.

This process removes the guesswork of "when." The system watches, scores, and tells the trader when conditions are ripe.

Tickers flow through a pipeline based on their scoring trajectory:

  • Setup — Building a case. Early signs of a potential move are forming.
  • Enter — Time to act. Score and conditions align for a new position.
  • Hold — The trade thesis is intact. Stay the course.
  • Defend — Caution. The trade is under pressure — tighten stops or reduce exposure.
  • Trim — Take partial profits. Momentum is fading but the trend may continue.
  • Exit — Close the position. The thesis is invalidated.

The Active Trader dashboard arranges tickers into swim-lanes by their current action phase (Setup, Enter, Hold, etc.). Within each lane, cards are sorted by score — the strongest setups appear first. Each card shows the ticker symbol, score, direction (LONG/SHORT), and price. Click any card to open the detail view with a full chart, technical breakdown, and precise levels.

The Daily Brief is a pre-market summary generated each trading day. It highlights top-scoring setups, sector rotations, and notable changes from the previous session. Think of it as a game plan for the day — everything needed to walk in prepared.

The Investor Dashboard provides a portfolio-level view of the simulated portfolio and overall market health:

  • Portfolio Health — An aggregate score showing how well current positions align with the system's signals.
  • Sector Heatmap — Strength and weakness across all 11 GICS sectors at a glance.
  • Market Health — Breadth, volatility, and regime indicators that provide context for the broader environment.
  • Simulated Trades — A full record of trades the system has taken, with entry/exit levels and P&L.

The Investor Dashboard is designed for a higher-level view — understanding the "big picture" before drilling into individual tickers on the Active Trader page.
